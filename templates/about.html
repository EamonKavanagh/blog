About me

My name is Eamon Kavanagh. I was born and raised in New York and recently graduated from the University of British Columbia with a M.Sc in Mathematics. My thesis focused on a chemotaxis model that you can read a bit more about here. I graduated summa cum laude from McMaster with a B.Sc (Hon) in Mathematics.

Right now I'm pursuing a job in data science. I like the fact that it's a burgeoning industry with a lot of uncharted terrain, where the amount of information we can wring from data is limited purely by creativity. I'm inspired by the recent successes of Google Flu Trends and the Netflix Prize which all took novel approaches (search result trends, crowd sourcing) to solving problems. Even in Google Flu Trends more recent failings or Netflix's decision to not implement the winning algorithm there's a lot to be learned. How much can we really learn when the data we source evolves to our very predictions? How do we build a robust system that can handle the growing amount of information we would ideally like to throw at it? I would love to not only explore the questions data science can answer but also questions about the how far the field can take us.

In my free time I like to play squash, take of my plants, read, make pizza dough and go on the occasionally bike tour. In the summer of 2011 I rode a bicycle across the United States with a good friend of mine. We kept a photo journal of the trip which you can read about here.
