About me

My name is Eamon Kavanagh. I was born and raised in New York and recently graduated from the University of British Columbia with a M.Sc in Mathematics where my thesis focused on a chemotaxis model that you can read a bit more about here. I graduated summa cum laude from McMaster with a B.Sc (Hon) in Mathematics.

Right now I'm pursuing a career in data science. I like the fact that it's a burgeoning industry with a lot of uncharted terrain, where the amount of information we can wring from data is limited purely by creativity. I'm motivated by the recent successes of Palantir, Google Flu Trends and the Netflix Prize which all took novel approaches (fraud detection, search result trends, crowd sourcing) to solve complex problems. Even in Google Flu Trends more recent failings or Netflix's decision to not implement the winning algorithm there's a lot to be learned. How much can we really say when the data we source evolves to our very predictions? How do we build a robust system that can handle the growing amount of information we would ideally like to throw at it? These high-profile setbacks have inspired me to think critically about the field and its possibilities. The power of the tool is obvious but how do we make it work for us?

In my free time I like to play squash, take care of my plants, read, make pizza dough and go on the occasional bike tour. In the summer of 2011 I rode a bicycle across the United States with a good friend of mine. We kept a photo journal of the trip which you can read about here.
